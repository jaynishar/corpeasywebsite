# CorpEasy Backlink Acquisition Strategy

## What Are Backlinks and Why They Matter

A **backlink** is when another website links to your website. Google treats each backlink as a "vote of confidence" — the more quality websites that link to you, the more authoritative Google considers your site, and the higher you rank.

Your competitors (WeWork, Executive Centre, Smartworks) rank on page 1 partly because they have **hundreds of backlinks** from authoritative sources. CorpEasy currently has very few, which is why you are "nowhere" in search results despite good technical SEO.

---

## How Backlinks Work

1. **Another website** publishes content that includes a link to corpeasy.in
2. **Google's crawler** discovers that link and follows it to your site
3. **Google's algorithm** counts this as a signal that your site is trustworthy/authoritative
4. **Your rankings improve** for relevant keywords over time (takes 2-12 weeks per link)

## How to Track Your Backlinks

Use these free/freemium tools:
1. **Google Search Console** (free) — Shows who links to you under "Links" > "Top linking sites"
2. **Ahrefs Webmaster Tools** (free) — ahrefs.com/webmaster-tools — Shows all backlinks
3. **Ubersuggest** (free tier) — neilpatel.com/ubersuggest — Backlink checker
4. **Moz Link Explorer** (free tier) — moz.com/link-explorer — Domain authority and backlinks

---

## What NOT to Do

- **Do NOT buy bulk backlinks** from Fiverr or similar services — these are low-quality and can get you penalized by Google
- **Do NOT use link farms or PBNs** (Private Blog Networks) — Google detects and penalizes these
- **Do NOT spam comment sections** with your link — these are "nofollow" links that do not help rankings
- **Do NOT exchange links excessively** with the same sites — looks manipulative to Google

## About the Excel Sheet

The Excel file you mentioned ("Rough sheet.xlsx") likely contains a list of specific websites where your competitors have backlinks but CorpEasy does not. To use it effectively:

1. **Open the file and identify columns** — It likely has: Source URL, Domain Authority, Competitor Has Link (Yes/No), CorpEasy Has Link (Yes/No)
2. **Sort by Domain Authority** (highest first) — Prioritize high-DA sites
3. **For each missing backlink:**
   - Visit the website
   - Find their "Contact", "Advertise", "Write for Us", or "Submit" page
   - Follow their process to get listed
   - If it is a directory, submit your business
   - If it is a blog, pitch a guest post
   - If it is a news site, send a press release

**I cannot directly add backlinks for you** — backlinks must be earned through the processes described above. No one can "add" your site to another website without their permission.
